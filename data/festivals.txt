Diwali:
- Festival of Lights
- Celebrates victory of good over evil
- Associated with Lord Rama’s return to Ayodhya
- People light diyas, decorate homes, burst crackers

Holi:
- Festival of Colours
- Celebrates love between Radha & Krishna
- Symbolizes the arrival of spring
- People apply colors and dance

Navratri:
- 9-day festival dedicated to Goddess Durga
- Garba and Dandiya dances are famous
- Each day is dedicated to a different form of Durga

Eid-ul-Fitr:
- Celebrated by Muslims after Ramadan fasting
- Special prayer at mosques & sharing of sweets

Christmas:
- Birth of Jesus Christ
- Decorating Christmas trees and exchanging gifts

Onam:
- Harvest festival of Kerala
- Celebrates King Mahabali
- Famous for boat races & pookalam (flower rangoli)

Pongal:
- Tamil Nadu harvest festival
- Thanks the Sun God for good harvest

Ganesh Chaturthi:
- Celebrates birth of Lord Ganesha
- Originated in Maharashtra
- Large public pandals, immersion (Visarjan)

Baisakhi:
- Sikh New Year & harvest festival
- Marks formation of Khalsa by Guru Gobind Singh

Durga Puja:
- Celebrated mainly in West Bengal
- Honors Goddess Durga’s victory over Mahishasura
