1. INTRODUCTION
- India adopted its Constitution on 26 January 1950.
- Longest written constitution in the world.

2. FEATURES
- Federal structure with unitary spirit.
- Parliamentary form of government.
- Secular, democratic republic.
- Independent judiciary.
- Fundamental Rights & Duties.
- Universal adult franchise.

3. FUNDAMENTAL RIGHTS
- Right to Equality
- Right to Freedom
- Right against Exploitation
- Right to Freedom of Religion
- Cultural & Educational Rights
- Right to Constitutional Remedies

4. FUNDAMENTAL DUTIES
- Respect constitution, national flag, national anthem.
- Protect environment.
- Promote harmony.
- Safeguard public property.

5. DIRECTIVE PRINCIPLES
- Social welfare, equal pay, health care, education.

6. GOVERNMENT STRUCTURE
- Legislature (Parliament)
- Executive (President, PM, Ministers)
- Judiciary (Supreme Court, High Courts)

7. IMPORTANT AMENDMENTS
- 42nd Amendment: changes to Constitution and powers (historical context).
- 44th Amendment: Emergency safeguards and reforms.
- 73rd & 74th: Panchayati Raj and urban local bodies.
